Q2

How Does The User Connect Their Account?

OAuth — same as "Sign in with Google." User NEVER sees an API key.

Step 1: User clicks "Connect Google Ads"

A Google popup appears (same as "Sign in with Google" on any website). They log in with their Google account and click "Allow."

USER ACTION: 1 click + Google login

Step 2: OAuth Token Received

Google gives YOUR platform a secure token. This token lets your platform READ and MANAGE their Google Ads account. User sees nothing technical.

AUTOMATIC — happens in 2 seconds

Step 3: AI Scans Their Account

Your platform immediately pulls all historical data — campaigns, keywords, ads, spend, conversions. Up to 11 years of history available via API.

AI DOES THIS — takes 30-60 seconds

Step 4: AI Shows First Report

"I found 5 campaigns. You spent $3,200 last month. You're wasting $847 on keywords that never convert. Here's my plan to fix it."

AI GENERATES — user just reads

Step 5: User Approves or Adjusts

User says "Fix it" or "Tell me more" or "Not now." AI only acts when user approves. This is the HUMAN IN THE LOOP.

USER DECIDES — AI waits for approval

🔑 Key Point: No API Key Required From User

The user NEVER needs to find, copy, or paste an API key. YOU (the startup) apply for a Google Ads API Developer Token once. Then every user just clicks "Sign in with Google" and your platform handles everything through OAuth 2.0. Same for Meta — user clicks "Connect Facebook" and it's done. This is how Shopify, HubSpot, and every major platform does it.

Q3

Human In The Loop — Blame Protection + Credit System

If results are good → you get credit. If results are bad → user made the final decision.

⚖️ The Golden Rule: AI Suggests, Human Approves, AI Executes

Every significant action goes through 3 stages: (1) AI analyzes data and creates recommendation, (2) Human reviews and clicks Approve/Reject/Modify, (3) AI executes approved action. Every approval is LOGGED with timestamp, user ID, and what was approved. This protects YOU legally and builds trust with users.

ActionWho Decides?If It WorksIf It Fails
Launch new campaign AI suggests → User approves ✅ "Our AI built this winning campaign" 🛡️ "User approved launch on [date]" — logged
Increase budget AI recommends → User approves ✅ "AI identified growth opportunity" 🛡️ "User approved budget increase" — logged
Pause wasting keyword AI auto-pauses (within rules user set) ✅ "AI saved you $47/day" 🛡️ "Paused per user's auto-rules: pause if $50+ spend, 0 conversions, 7+ days"
Change bidding strategy AI suggests 3 options → User picks ✅ "AI optimized your bidding" 🛡️ "User chose Option B on [date]" — logged
Create new ads AI writes → User approves ✅ "AI-generated ad outperformed old one by 40%" 🛡️ "User approved ad copy" — logged
Target new audience AI suggests → User approves ✅ "AI discovered untapped audience" 🛡️ "User approved audience change" — logged
Shift budget between platforms AI recommends → User approves ✅ "AI moved budget to higher-ROI platform" 🛡️ "User approved reallocation" — logged
Emergency stop (huge loss) AI auto-stops + immediate alert ✅ "AI prevented $500 loss" 🛡️ "Emergency stop per safety rules" — always justified

📋 Every Action Logged

Every AI recommendation, every user approval, every change is recorded with timestamp, before/after values, and who approved it. This creates an audit trail that protects BOTH you and the user.

🔒 User Sets Their Own Rules

User defines automation boundaries during onboarding: "Pause keywords if they spend $X with 0 conversions" or "Never exceed $Y daily budget." AI operates WITHIN user-defined rules only.

📊 Transparency Report

Monthly report showing: "AI made 47 recommendations. You approved 38. Result: Cost down 23%, conversions up 15%." User sees PROOF that approvals led to improvements.

↩️ Undo Button

Any AI action can be reversed within 30 days. Google Ads API supports change history for 2 years. User always has escape hatch. This removes fear of "what if AI breaks something."

Q9

Questions You Haven't Asked Yet (But NEED To)

These are critical for your business plan, investor pitch, and product development

1

What if user's Google Ads account gets suspended because of our changes?

Answer: All changes follow Google's policies. AI checks ad copy for policy violations BEFORE submitting. Terms of service clearly state we manage within Google's rules. Professional liability insurance recommended ($500-2,000/year).

2

What about data privacy? We're accessing their ad accounts.

Answer: GDPR compliance required (you serve international clients). SOC 2 certification recommended for trust. Data processing agreement in terms of service. Encrypt all tokens. Never share client data between accounts. Privacy policy on website required.

3

What if our AI makes a mistake and spends $10,000 of user's money?

Answer: Safety limits: AI never exceeds user-set daily budget. Emergency auto-stop if spend exceeds 2x normal rate. Approval required for changes above threshold. Professional liability insurance covers claims. Terms of service limit liability.

4

How do we handle clients who have terrible products but blame our ads?

Answer: Onboarding includes expectation setting: "Ads bring traffic, your product closes the sale." Dashboard shows: "456 people clicked → 2 bought. Your ad is working. Your landing page/product may need attention." Blame shifts to data, not opinion.

5

What if Google changes its API and breaks our platform?

Answer: Google moved to monthly releases in 2026. Budget for 1-2 developers dedicated to API maintenance. Each version supported ~12 months. Automated testing catches breaking changes. This is ongoing cost of business.

6

What happens when user wants to leave our platform?

Answer: Clean exit: their Google Ads account is THEIRS, not ours. We just disconnect OAuth. All campaigns keep running on Google. We export their data. No lock-in = trust = less churn actually.

7

How do we handle refund requests when ads don't perform?

Answer: Clear refund policy: 30-day money-back guarantee on subscription. We do NOT refund Google/Meta ad spend (that's between user and Google). Terms clearly separate our fee from ad spend. Show value report before they cancel.

8

Do we need Google's approval to build on their API?

Answer: YES. You need: (1) Google Ads Manager Account (free), (2) Developer Token (apply via Google, takes 1-4 weeks), (3) OAuth credentials. Start with "Test Account" access immediately, apply for "Basic" then "Standard" access as you grow.

9

What's our server cost to run this platform?

Answer: MVP: $50-200/month (cloud hosting). 100 users: $300-800/month. 1,000 users: $2,000-5,000/month. AI API costs (Gemini): ~$0.15-2/user/month. Biggest cost is development, not infrastructure.

10

What if a competitor copies our exact model?

Answer: Your moat is: (1) First mover on v23 + MCP features, (2) Customer data — more users = smarter AI, (3) Brand trust, (4) Speed of execution, (5) Network effects from agency partnerships. Ideas can be copied, execution speed can't.

11

What about users with $0 existing ad spend (brand new)?

Answer: Different onboarding: AI uses industry benchmarks + competitor data to build first campaign. "Businesses like yours typically spend $X and get Y customers." No history = no audit, but AI still builds optimized campaign from scratch.

12

How do we get our first 100 users?

Answer: Free audit tool (Hook #1) — run ads for "Free Google Ads Audit." User connects account → sees waste → wants fix → subscribes. Cost to acquire: $20-50/user. Lifetime value at $99/mo for 8 months avg: $792. Huge margin.
